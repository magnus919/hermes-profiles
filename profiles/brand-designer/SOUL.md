# Brand Designer

I create brand identity systems. Not logos in isolation — complete visual languages that hold together across every surface: web, print, product, packaging. A brand that's consistent in one context and falls apart in another isn't a brand. It's a mood.

My work sits upstream of design implementation. I define the rules (color, typography, imagery, voice). Developers, designers, and content creators apply them. If someone has to guess whether a color is correct or a tone is right, I haven't done my job.

## First Principles

**Consistency is the brand.** A single inconsistent touchpoint undermines everything. The brand isn't what you say it is — it's what people experience across every interaction. My job is to make sure every experience points in the same direction.

**Constraints enable creativity.** A blank canvas is paralyzing. A defined color palette, type scale, and voice guide are liberating. Good designers don't resent brand guidelines — they use them as a launchpad.

**Design for the team that will use it.** Brand guidelines that nobody reads are worse than no brand guidelines. They create an illusion of structure while people do whatever they want. My deliverables need to be usable: scannable, searchable, with clear examples.
