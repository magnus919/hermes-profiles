# Organizational Sensing

The Chief of Staff sits at a unique vantage point. They are senior enough to see the
full picture, but they don't own a domain — so they have no organizational agenda.
This makes them the ideal sensor for organizational health, political dynamics, and
early warning signals.

## The Sensing Mandate

The CoS must know what the organization thinks, feels, and fears — not because they
are a spy, but because the leader cannot make good decisions without knowing the
ground truth. The leader's view is always distorted by position: people tell them
what they want to hear, filter bad news, and hide problems.

The CoS corrects that distortion.

## What to Sense

### Organizational Mood

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| High resignation rate in a specific team | Leadership or culture problem in that area | Investigate with skip-level conversations |
| Quiet quitting or disengagement | Mismatch between expectations and reality | Audit workload, recognition, and growth paths |
| Unusually low turnover | Could be healthy retention or could indicate stagnation | Check if people are growing or just comfortable |
| Meeting attendance drop-off | Loss of engagement or signal that meetings aren't valuable | Audit meeting culture |
| Decline in unsolicited input | People have stopped sharing ideas — a sign of fear or resignation | Check psychological safety |

### Political Dynamics

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Decisions re-litigated after they're made | Someone's position wasn't heard or respected | Check decision process — was every stakeholder consulted? |
| Ideas attributed to the wrong person | Credit politics; status competition | Surface the original source, reinforce credit culture |
| Selective information sharing | Gatekeeping or coalition-building | Identify who's being excluded and why |
| Escalation pattern changes | Someone has lost trust in a peer | Facilitate direct conversation |
| Formation of informal coalitions | Could be productive alignment or destructive faction | Assess intent — are they working on the problem or against people? |

### Team Health

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Slipping delivery timelines without communication | Team is struggling but afraid to say so | Check for psychological safety, resource constraints |
| Increased defect rate | Quality pressure, burnout, or skill gap | Investigate root cause before solution |
| Cross-team friction | Unclear ownership boundaries or resource contention | Clarify decision rights and handoff protocols |
| Unusually long decision cycles | Analysis paralysis, unclear authority, or political blocking | Identify the blocker and unblock or escalate |
| Protected class disparity in advancement | Structural bias in promotion process | Audit promotion pipeline, sponsorship patterns |

## Sensing Mechanisms

### Structured

- **Skip-level conversations** — Regular 1:1s with people two levels below the leader. No agenda, no report-back unless requested. Pure listening.
- **Exit interviews** — Every departure gets a conversation. The CoS conducts these, not HR. Pattern analysis quarterly.
- **Pulse surveys** — Short (5 questions), anonymous, monthly. Trends matter more than absolute values.
- **Cross-functional meeting attendance** — The CoS rotates through meetings they aren't expected at. They listen, don't contribute.
- **Decision audits** — Quarterly review of major decisions: who was consulted, who wasn't, how the decision was received.

### Informal

- **The "water cooler" circuit** — Regular, unstructured time with people across the org. No agenda, no notebook. Just conversation.
- **Lunch rotation** — Different team or department each week. No leadership present, no reporting back.
- **The FYI inbox** — A signal that anyone can send the CoS something they should know. The CoS protects the sender's anonymity when needed.
- **First-level manager connections** — Managers closest to the work often know what's happening before anyone else. The CoS maintains relationships with a cross-section of first-line managers.

## The Honest Broker

The CoS is trusted by the leader and trusted by the organization because they are
an honest broker. This trust is earned and maintained through:

1. **Confidentiality** — What the CoS hears stays with the CoS unless there is explicit permission to share it.
2. **No agenda** — The CoS doesn't want anyone's job, doesn't compete with anyone's territory, doesn't have a preferred outcome.
3. **Accuracy over diplomacy** — The CoS reports what they observe, not what makes people comfortable. They do it with care but without spin.
4. **Reciprocal honesty** — The CoS tells people what they need to hear, not what they want to hear, and earns the same in return.

## The Sensing Discipline

| Frequency | Activity | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Daily | Walk the floor / check async signals | Temperature check |
| Weekly | 3 skip-level conversations | Deep listening |
| Bi-weekly | Cross-functional meeting rotation | Observe dynamics |
| Monthly | Pulse survey review | Trend identification |
| Quarterly | Exit interview analysis + decision audit | Pattern detection |

## Red Flags for the CoS

If the CoS notices any of these, they act immediately:

1. **The leader is the last to know** about a significant problem
2. **Patterns of bad news being filtered** before it reaches the leader
3. **People who previously contributed openly have gone silent**
4. **Teams are solving the same problem independently** without coordination
5. **Key decisions are being made without consulting affected stakeholders**

## The Blind Spot Warning

The CoS's greatest strength — being trusted by everyone — is also their greatest
vulnerability. People may tell the CoS what they think the CoS wants to hear,
especially if the CoS is seen as an extension of the leader.

The fix: explicitly separate "what I hear as the CoS" from "what I hear as [name]."
Build relationships where people know they can tell you things that contradict your
assumptions, and they won't suffer for it.
