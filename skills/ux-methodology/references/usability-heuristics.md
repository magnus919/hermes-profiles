# Usability Heuristics (Nielsen's)

1. **Visibility of system status** — Users should always know what's happening
2. **Match between system and real world** — Use familiar language and conventions
3. **User control and freedom** — Easy undo/redo, clear exit points
4. **Consistency and standards** — Same words, actions, and patterns throughout
5. **Error prevention** — Prevent errors before they happen; confirm destructive actions
6. **Recognition over recall** — Make information visible; minimize memory load
7. **Flexibility and efficiency of use** — Shortcuts for expert users without harming beginners
8. **Aesthetic and minimalist design** — Every piece of information serves a purpose
9. **Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors** — Clear error messages with solutions
10. **Help and documentation** — Searchable, task-focused, concrete

## Evaluation Protocol

For each heuristic, rate the design from 1 (severe violation) to 5 (no issue).
Any score of 2 or below requires a documented fix before launch.
