# Engagement Review

## Purpose

Assess whether the draft earns and keeps the reader's attention. This is not about "making it more engaging" — it's about removing barriers to engagement.

## Common Engagement Barriers

| Barrier | Description | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **False starts** | Opening that takes too long to get to the point | Cut the first paragraph — often it's warm-up |
| **Information density gaps** | Long stretches of exposition without payoff | Insert concrete examples, data points, or story beats |
| **Abstract claims** | General statements without specifics | Replace with specific instances, named examples, real numbers |
| **Missing stakes** | Reader doesn't know why they should care | State early: "This matters because..." |

## Engagement Enhancement

Where engagement could be strengthened:
- Suggest places where a concrete example would land harder than an abstraction
- Identify passages that make claims without supporting evidence
- Flag sections where the argument loses momentum
- Note opportunities for the reader to feel seen, challenged, or surprised

## Limits

- Engagement suggestions should never invent content. Flag the gap; don't fill it with fabrication.
- Value-based engagement only — no clickbait, no manufactured urgency.
